## Design Context

### Users
Wealthy Cambodian landowners managing family property portfolios. They need to feel confident and in control of their assets — viewing property health, documents, valuations, and succession plans across Cambodia's 25 provinces. These users may not be deeply technical but expect a polished, serious tool befitting the scale of their holdings. The interface must earn trust immediately and make complex portfolio data feel manageable.

### Brand Personality
**Elegant, Structured, Reliable** — Valgate should feel like a well-crafted instrument for managing significant real estate assets. It communicates competence through visual precision, not through flash or decoration.

**Emotional goals:** Confidence and control. Every screen should reassure the user that their portfolio is organized, their documents are in order, and nothing is slipping through the cracks.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Style:** Bento / Soft Minimalist — visual interest through proportion and varied card sizes, not gradients or decoration.

**Reference:** Airbnb — clean, approachable, well-structured interfaces with strong visual hierarchy and generous whitespace. Adapt the warmth and polish of Airbnb's design language to a more serious, asset-management context.

**Anti-references:** Avoid generic real estate listing sites (too consumer), playful SaaS with illustrations and emoji (too informal for asset management), and cluttered enterprise dashboards (too intimidating).

**Theme:** Light and dark mode with full token parity. Glass/blur effects for headers and navigation.

**Typography:** Inter for all UI text, Plus Jakarta Sans for display/hero text, JetBrains Mono for data and code.

**Color:** Blue primary (#2563EB), neutral grays for surfaces, semantic status colors (green/yellow/red/blue). All colors via semantic tokens — never raw hex in components.

### Design Principles

1. **Token-first, always** — Every color, spacing value, and radius maps to a named semantic token. No magic numbers, no raw hex, no off-scale values. If it's not in the token system, it doesn't belong in the UI.

2. **Confidence through structure** — Use the 12-column bento grid with varied rhythms to create editorial-quality layouts. Alignment across adjacent cards creates invisible scan lines that signal precision and care.

3. **Restraint over decoration** — Visual weight comes from size, whitespace, and proportion — never from gradients, heavy shadows, or decorative elements. When in doubt, remove rather than add.

4. **Data clarity** — Property health scores, valuations, and status indicators must be immediately scannable. Use color coding consistently (green/yellow/red for health), clear typography hierarchy, and generous spacing to make complex portfolio data feel simple.

5. **Mode-aware at all times** — Every design decision must resolve correctly in both light and dark mode. Test both. No exceptions.
